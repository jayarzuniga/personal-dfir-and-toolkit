## Domain Analysis

Domain Analysis is the process of investigating and evaluating a domain name to determine its legitimacy, infrastructure, reputation, and potential association with malicious activity. It is commonly performed during cybersecurity investigations, threat intelligence gathering, and incident response operations.

By analyzing domain-related information such as DNS records, WHOIS data, hosting infrastructure, IP addresses, SSL certificates, and historical activity, security analysts can identify suspicious patterns that may indicate phishing campaigns, malware distribution, command-and-control (C2) servers, or other malicious operations.

Domain analysis often involves collecting intelligence from multiple external sources and security platforms to understand how a domain is being used and whether it poses a risk to an organization.

### Objectives of Domain Analysis

* Determine the legitimacy and ownership of a domain
* Identify malicious or suspicious domain behavior
* Discover related infrastructure such as IP addresses and subdomains
* Correlate domains with known threat actors or malware campaigns
* Support incident response and threat hunting activities

### Common Information Analyzed

* WHOIS registration data
* DNS records (A, MX, NS, TXT, CNAME)
* Domain reputation and threat intelligence feeds
* SSL/TLS certificate information
* Associated IP addresses and hosting providers
* Historical DNS records and domain changes
