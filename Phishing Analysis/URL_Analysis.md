## URL Analysis

URL Analysis is the process of examining and evaluating a Uniform Resource Locator (URL) to determine its safety, legitimacy, and potential association with malicious activity. It is a fundamental technique in cybersecurity investigations, phishing detection, and threat intelligence operations, as malicious URLs are commonly used to deliver malware, redirect victims to phishing pages, or establish command-and-control communications.

By analyzing URLs through multiple intelligence platforms and sandbox environments, security analysts can identify obfuscated links, decode shortened URLs, detect typosquatting or domain spoofing, and correlate URLs with known threat campaigns.

### Objectives of URL Analysis

* Determine whether a URL is malicious, suspicious, or benign
* Decode shortened or obfuscated URLs to reveal their true destination
* Identify phishing pages, malware distribution sites, and C2 infrastructure
* Correlate URLs with known threat actors, campaigns, and indicators of compromise
* Support incident response, threat hunting, and user-reported phishing investigations

### Common Information Analyzed

* Full URL structure including protocol, domain, path, and parameters
* Domain reputation and threat intelligence feed results
* Screenshot and visual rendering of the URL destination
* Redirect chains and final landing page
* Associated IP addresses and hosting infrastructure
* URL categorization and web filtering classifications
* DNS twist and typosquatting variations
